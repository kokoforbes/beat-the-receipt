Why support the Paperless Pledge?

In accordance with the United Nations’ Sustainable Development Goals, most world governments have committed to becoming a carbon neutral nation by 2050. As the largest private sector, the retail industry must take drastic steps to decarbonise its products, stores, supply chains and operations if the world is going to successfully achieve net-zero.

More than 80 million paper receipts are printed every single day in the world, generating over 750,000 tonnes of carbon each year. It’s the unseen environmental issue that no-one’s talking about.

It’s not a legal requirement to provide customers with a paper receipt when they transact in-store. Plus, more than half of shoppers surveyed in 2021 say they want the option to refuse a paper receipt at the till - so why are we still printing them automatically for every transaction?

The retail industry has already made significant progress in reducing carbon emissions. Switching to optional receipt printing is one of the fastest, simplest ways to cut your annual emissions and reduce recycling and refuse pressures.

So what are you waiting for? Take the Paperless Pledge today and help us #DitchTheReceipt!

Support the Paperless Pledge as a Consumer

Our supermarkets process millions of transactions every week. Although great progress has been made to reduce the number of paper receipts being printed - like the introduction of opt-in receipts at self-checkouts - there’s still a long way to go.

Add your voice to the campaign by signing our letter to the supermarkets, below.

Dear supermarkets

I’m reaching out today to thank you for taking steps to reduce the number of paper receipts being printed in your stores.

We cut down hundreds of thousands of trees each year to produce paper receipts - many of which can’t be recycled safely due to toxic chemicals embedded in the thermal paper.

But most importantly, paper receipts emit 2.5Kg of CO2e throughout their lifetime. That’s more than 750,000 tonnes of carbon leaching into the atmosphere every year for the sake of these tiny pieces of paper - most of which end up lost or thrown away.

As we work towards our goal of a net-zero future, I’m asking you to sign the Paperless Pledge and commit to making paper receipts optional in all of your stores by 2023. By taking the sustainable option by default you’ll ensure that receipts are only printed for those of us who actually need them - which not only helps all of us reduce our carbon footprint, but saves you money, too!

Thank you for the progress you’ve made so far and for your consideration.
